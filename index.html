Abstract

      The study of galaxy formation throughout history has played a huge role in understanding our own galaxy, but the structure of the universe itself, with some of the earliest galaxies being formed around 300-400 million years ago after the Big Bang (Williams, 2025). 
      Its study has enabled scientists to explain the factors that contribute to it, such as galaxy collisions, which are capable of either tearing galaxies apart or merging them into larger galaxies (Sabia, 2023). 
      This paper aims to evaluate the effectiveness of hydrodynamical models in studying galaxy formation, which are well known and reliable computational methods used in this particular area of astrophysics (Joop Schaye, 2023) . To investigate the premise,
      the model FLAMINGO, a model developed by researches at the university of leiden, was chosen, where it was evaluated based on its accuracy in calculating stellar masses, how effective it was in reproducing galaxy clusters and how big its reliable data range was.
